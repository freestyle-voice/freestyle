## Dictate with the hotkey

The default hotkey is **Option+Space** on macOS and **Alt+Space** on Windows and Linux. There are two ways to use it:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Hold (push-to-talk)" icon="hand-pointer">
    Hold to record, release to transcribe. Great for quick bursts.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Toggle" icon="toggle-on">
    Press once to start, press again to stop. Great for longer dictation.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

To change it, go to **Settings → Recording**. Pick **Hold** or **Toggle** under **Activation**, and click the current shortcut to rebind it (it needs a modifier key or a side mouse button).

Under **Output mode** you can choose **Paste into app** (text is typed straight into the active window) or **Copy to clipboard** (you paste it yourself). Clipboard mode helps with apps where auto-paste misbehaves.

<Note>
  On macOS, paste needs **Accessibility** permission. On Linux it uses `xdotool` or `wtype`. If nothing shows up, check **Settings → Permissions**.
</Note>

## Pick a model

Freestyle runs local models on your device or connects to cloud providers with your own API key. Manage both under **Settings → Models**.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Freestyle Transcribe">
    The all in one transcription service built by the Freestyle team. We've tweaked it to work particularly well for developers. Works immediately when you log in. 

    - Great if you have a weaker PC that can't run local models well. 
    - Transcription and post processing all in one, runs super fast. 
    - 0 day retention by default. All transcriptions are private and not stored. 

  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Local (on-device)">
    Models run locally. Your audio never leaves your computer.

    - **whisper.cpp** runs everywhere. The *Balanced* model is a solid default.
    - **Qwen3** Great model for MLX Apple Silicon.
    - **SenseVoice** Also a decent model. 
    - **Parakeet** Great open source model from NVIDIA.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Cloud (your own key)">
    Often faster and more accurate, but needs internet and a key. Supported providers: **OpenAI**, **Groq**, **Anthropic**, **Google**, **Deepgram**, and **ElevenLabs**.

    1. Open **Settings → Models** and pick a provider.
    2. Paste your API key. Keys live in your system keychain, never logged or sent to us.
    3. Select the model to make it your default.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Clean up with AI

Raw speech is messy. Freestyle can run your transcript through a language model to tidy it up. Set this under **Settings → Models** after you pick an LLM model (separate from your voice model).

Choose a **cleanup intensity**:

| Level | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Low** | Fixes punctuation and obvious slips. Keeps your exact words. |
| **Medium** | Cleans grammar and phrasing without changing meaning. |
| **High** | Rephrases and reorders for flow, keeping your meaning. |
| **Custom** | Write your own instructions. |

For **Custom**, hit **View / edit prompt** and say exactly how you want text rewritten. Editing any preset switches you to Custom so the built-ins stay put.

## Build a dictionary

The **Dictionary** (in the sidebar) swaps short spoken phrases for longer text. Say the key, get the value.

For example, `my address` could expand to your full street address, or `type script` could become `TypeScript`.

To add one, open **Dictionary**, click **Add entry**, type the phrase as the **key** and the replacement as the **value**, and save. It works right away. You can search entries and import or export them as JSON.

## Sharpen recognition with vocabulary

The **Vocabulary** page helps the model recognize tricky words like names, brands, and jargon *before* transcription. Add a term plus an optional note.

Quick way to remember the difference: vocabulary changes *how audio is heard*, the dictionary rewrites text *after*.

<Note>
  Vocabulary support varies by model. Some handle multi-word phrases, some only boost single words, and a few skip it entirely. The page tells you what your model supports.
</Note>

## Format for the app you're in

**Formats** reshape your transcript based on *where* you're typing. Dictate into Gmail and get an email, dictate into Slack and stay casual.

To add one, open **Formats**, click **Add format**, then:

1. Give it a **label** like "Email".
2. Set an **app pattern** that matches the app name, URL, or window title. Patterns are pipe-separated and case-insensitive, like `mail.google.com | outlook | Spark`.
3. Write the **instructions**, like "Format as a professional email with a greeting, body, and sign-off."

Freestyle ships with defaults you can edit. Editing one makes it a custom rule (it gets a **CUSTOM** badge). **Reset to defaults** undoes your changes.
