Why dictation should run on your laptop, not in the cloud

We built Freestyle local-first on purpose. Here's the case for keeping your voice on your own machine — and what it took to make on-device transcription feel instant.

Matteo Pignatelli
Founder · Freestyle May 24, 2026 · 7 min read Share

Most voice apps ship your audio to a server, run a model there, and send text back. It works — until your connection drops, or you stop to think about who else can hear what you just said.

We took the other path. Freestyle transcribes on your machine by default, so your voice never has to leave it. That decision shaped everything: the latency budget, the model sizes we could use, even the shape of the recording indicator. This post is about why we made it, and the engineering it took to make “local” feel as fast as “cloud.”

The cloud tax

Round-tripping audio to a data center adds a tax you pay on every single dictation: the upload, the queue, the inference, the download. On a good connection that's a few hundred milliseconds. On a train, it's a spinner. Either way, your words sit on someone else's hardware — and that's the part that bothered us most.

  • Privacy by default. Audio is processed where it's recorded. Nothing is uploaded unless you explicitly choose a cloud model.
  • Works offline. On a plane, in a basement, behind a corporate firewall — dictation still works.
  • No account, no telemetry. You download the app and start talking.
“The fastest network request is the one you never make.”
— design principle no. 1

Making on-device feel instant

The catch with local-first is that you're now bound by the user's hardware, not a rack of GPUs. We leaned on quantized Whisper variants, streamed partial results as you speak, and started transcription the moment you press fn — not when you release it. By the time you let go, most of the work is already done.

What we measured

After switching the default path to on-device, median time-to-paste dropped below the point most people notice. The text just appears where your cursor is, the way it should. Below: a side-by-side of the pipeline before and after.

Where the cloud still helps

Local-first doesn't mean local-only. Some jobs — very long recordings, niche languages, the highest-accuracy models — are still better in the cloud. So Freestyle lets you bring your own model and route to a provider per use case. The default stays private; the choice stays yours.

That's the whole philosophy in one line: your voice, your machine, your call.
